Purpose & Scope

This page outlines the operating principles, ethical commitments, and professional boundaries under which Guardian Core conducts its security operations and advisory services.

These principles apply to all engagements, assessments, advisory activities, and public-facing services provided by Guardian Core.

Operating Principles

Guardian Core aligns its operating principles with the CIA triad—Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability—which is widely used within the information security profession to guide responsible risk management and control design.

References to industry frameworks, certifications, or professional bodies are provided for context only and do not imply endorsement, certification authority, or sponsorship.

Confidentiality

Guardian Core is committed to protecting information against unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse.

  • Access to information is limited to the minimum necessary to achieve agreed objectives.
  • Sensitive information is handled deliberately and disclosed only to authorized parties.
  • Secure communication and transfer methods are used where appropriate.
  • Client information is treated as confidential unless explicitly agreed otherwise.

Integrity

Guardian Core is committed to preserving the accuracy, completeness, and reliability of systems, data, and security-related decisions.

  • Findings and recommendations are evidence-based and clearly documented.
  • Assumptions, limitations, and constraints are stated transparently.
  • Changes are recommended in a manner that minimizes unintended operational impact.
  • Deliverables are prepared to support audit, governance, and executive review.

Availability

Guardian Core recognizes that security controls must support the availability and reliability of business systems and services.

  • Security recommendations consider resilience, recoverability, and continuity.
  • Controls are designed to reduce single points of failure.
  • Operational usability is considered to reduce unsafe workarounds.
  • Security is positioned as an enabler of business operations, not an obstacle.

Authorization & Engagement Boundaries

Guardian Core performs security activities only within explicitly defined and authorized scope.

  • No testing, validation, or assessment is performed without documented authorization.
  • All activities are limited to the systems, environments, and objectives agreed in advance.
  • Guardian Core does not engage in unauthorized access, exploitation, or surveillance.
  • Any change to scope requires explicit agreement prior to execution.

Ethical & Professional Conduct

Guardian Core conducts its work in accordance with recognized professional ethics, applicable laws, and contractual obligations.

  • Work is performed responsibly, lawfully, and in good faith.
  • Findings are communicated discreetly and to appropriate stakeholders only.
  • Conflicts of interest are avoided or disclosed where applicable.
  • Recommendations prioritize risk reduction over unnecessary complexity.

Responsible Disclosure

Guardian Core encourages responsible disclosure of security issues related to its public web properties or services.

Reports should avoid public disclosure of sensitive information and include sufficient detail to support verification and remediation.

Privacy Notice

Guardian Core follows a minimal-data handling approach. Personal or sensitive information is collected only where necessary to fulfill an agreed engagement.

  • Information collection is limited to defined objectives.
  • Data retention is minimized and purpose-driven.
  • Secure handling methods are selected based on sensitivity.
  • Secure transfer options may be arranged when required.
