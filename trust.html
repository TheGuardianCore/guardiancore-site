Operating Principles

Guardian Core aligns its operating principles with the CIA triad—Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability—used throughout the security profession to guide responsible decision-making and control design.

Guardian Core’s principles are informed by recognized industry practices and professional ethics. References to third-party organizations are for context and do not imply endorsement.

Confidentiality

Protect information against unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse.

  • Use least-privilege access and minimum necessary data handling.
  • Limit collection, retention, and sharing of sensitive information.
  • Prefer secure channels for communication and file transfer.
  • Handle client information with discretion and professional care.

Integrity

Protect systems and data against unauthorized modification, manipulation, or destruction.

  • Provide evidence-based findings and clearly stated assumptions.
  • Maintain transparent change documentation and traceable decisions.
  • Recommend controls that reduce risk without introducing hidden fragility.
  • Preserve trust in the accuracy of reporting and the reliability of outcomes.

Availability

Ensure systems and services remain accessible and reliable for legitimate business needs.

  • Design security controls that support business continuity, not just restriction.
  • Prioritize resilience, recoverability, and operational readiness.
  • Reduce single points of failure and improve response preparedness.
  • Balance security with usability to avoid workarounds that increase risk.

Ethical & Professional Commitment

Guardian Core engages in security work responsibly and lawfully. Where testing, validation, or assessment activities are performed, they are conducted only with explicit authorization and defined scope.

  • Authorization-first: No testing outside agreed scope.
  • Responsibility: Findings are handled discreetly and shared with appropriate stakeholders.
  • Clarity: Recommendations are prioritized, practical, and aligned with real constraints.
  • Defensibility: Work products are documented to support leadership and audit scrutiny.

Responsible Disclosure

If you believe you have found a security issue related to Guardian Core services or public web properties, please report it responsibly. Provide clear reproduction details and avoid sharing sensitive information publicly.

Submit a responsible disclosure report

Please include: affected URL/system, steps to reproduce, impact assessment, and any supporting evidence.

Privacy & Data Handling

Guardian Core prefers security solutions that minimize sensitive data exposure. When client information must be reviewed to complete an engagement, handling is kept intentional, limited, and aligned with the engagement scope.

  • Collect only what is required to achieve the agreed outcome.
  • Keep data retention minimal and purpose-driven.
  • Use secure storage/transfer methods appropriate to sensitivity.
